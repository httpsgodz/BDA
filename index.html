Blinds Digital Assistant Portfolio

Introduction

Blinds Digital Assistant is an innovative mobile application developed to assist visually impaired individuals with daily tasks through the use of cutting-edge artificial intelligence (AI) technology. The app offers real-time navigation support, obstacle detection, and enhanced autonomy, empowering users to navigate their environment safely and confidently.

  • ISEF: Participated two consecutive years and reached the finals (2023/2024)
  • 24th Arab Conference: 2nd place (20 Oct 2024)
  • Intergenerational Innovation Conference 2024: 3rd place (29 Dec 2024)

Technologies & Tools

  • YOLOv3 (You Only Look Once version 3): A real-time object detection system used for identifying obstacles and objects in the user's environment, ensuring safe navigation.
  • OpenCV: Utilized for image processing capabilities, including detecting light levels and identifying objects, which enables the automatic adjustment of blinds based on environmental conditions or user preferences.
  • NumPy: Facilitates fast data processing for sensor inputs and image analysis, optimizing performance for tasks like object recognition and spatial awareness.

Background Research

Research in the field of visually impaired education and communication highlights a significant global challenge. Statistically, only 0.5% to 15% of the population experiences visual impairment. The World Health Organization reports that 35 million people are blind, and approximately 120 million are visually impaired. A major portion of these individuals resides in developing countries, where proper healthcare services are often inaccessible.

There are two types of blindness:

  • Congenital Blindness: Individuals who have been blind since birth or early childhood.
  • Acquired Blindness: Those who lose their vision later in life, often after education years. This type can affect memory retention and learning, particularly for those who haven't memorized key information before blindness.

Recommendations

  • The Blinds Digital Assistant provides innovative AI-powered assistance for the visually impaired, offering key functionalities:
    • Obstacle Detection: The app uses AI to detect obstacles and convert visual data into real-time auditory cues, helping users navigate their surroundings independently.
    • Enhanced Autonomy: The application ensures users’ safety by providing timely alerts for obstacles and helping them maneuver their environments more confidently.

Future Enhancements:

  • Support for Additional Languages: Expanding the language options for text recognition and audio support to reach a broader audience.
  • Improved Object Recognition: Future updates will introduce recognition for a wider range of objects, such as facial recognition and specific location identification.

Execution Plan & Timeline

  • Phase 1: Initial Research & Development (Nov 2022 – Mar 2023)
    • Conduct in-depth research to identify the needs of visually impaired users.
    • Develop an initial prototype with basic AI features.
    • Gather feedback from a small group of visually impaired individuals to improve the prototype.
  • Phase 2: Integration of Advanced Technologies (Apr 2023 – Jul 2023)
    • Implement YOLOv3 for real-time object detection and OpenCV for image processing and light detection.
    • Continue testing and refining the app’s features based on user feedback.
  • Phase 3: Expansion & Feature Improvement (Aug 2023 – Dec 2023)
    • Introduce multi-language support for better accessibility.
    • Expand object recognition capabilities to include a broader range of items and obstacles.
  • Phase 4: Beta Testing & User Feedback (Jan 2024 – May 2024)
    • Release the app for beta testing to a larger group of users.
    • Analyze beta feedback and make improvements in the UI/UX.
  • Phase 5: Official Launch & Conference Participation (Jun 2024 – Oct 2024)
    • Launch the official version of the app for public use.
    • Participate in tech and innovation conferences, including ICPC, ISEF, and the Arab Conference, to gain recognition and feedback.
  • Phase 6: Ongoing Improvements & Feature Updates (Nov 2024 – Present)
    • Continue to update the app with new features, bug fixes, and performance improvements.
    • Explore additional technologies such as facial recognition for enhanced object detection and support for more complex environments.
